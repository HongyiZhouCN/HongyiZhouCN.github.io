I am a PhD student in the Intuitive Robots Lab (IRL) at the Karlsruhe Institute of Technology (KIT), Germany. My research focuses on robotics and machine learning supervised by Rudolf Lioutikov. Before starting my PhD I obtained my Master Degree in Mechanical Engineering at the KIT where I wrote my thesis at Bosch supervised by Gerhard Neumann. During my studies I interned at Audi AG, IPG Automotive, and the Research Center for Informatics (FZI).

Research

My research interests lie in the field of imitation learning, where I focus on developing algorithms that enable robots to learn from human demonstrations to perform complex tasks. In particular, I am interested in exploring the potential of playdata and self-supervised techniques for scaling up learning from demonstrations. Additionally, I am fascinated by the application of diffusion models to robotics and how they can be leveraged to enhance robot learning.
